John-Luca Mascali's Response

Critical Atlas of the Internet by Louise Drulhe

The "Critical Atlas of the Internet" by Louise Drulhe offers a distinct insight into the ever-evolving landscape of the digital universe. Drulhe's analysis sheds light on the intricate bond between the tangible world and virtual territories.

What really stood out to me was Drulhe's emphasis on making the internet seem almost tangible, as if we could reach out and touch it. Her perspective challenges the typical way many of us view the vast expanse of the World Wide Web. Navigating her webpage's design, I was reminded of our diverse internet journeys. Each segment of her site is distinct, mirroring individual thoughts or ideas. Yet, they all seem to flow together, interconnected in an intricate web. This reflects our own experiences online: we might hop from one topic or site to another, but everything we encounter is part of a larger, interconnected universe. The very fact that we can relate and reflect based on our personal online experiences makes the internet's vastness feel closer to home, giving us the sense that we can truly understand and grasp its enormity. It's a powerful reminder that while our digital experiences might feel isolated at times, they're all part of a vast, intertwined digital landscape.
